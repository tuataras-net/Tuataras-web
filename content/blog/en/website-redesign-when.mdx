"My site looks old" isn't enough reason for a redesign. Sometimes it's necessary — but only when objective signals beat the noise.

## Real redesign signals

1. **Stagnant conversion** after several point optimizations.
2. **Lighthouse &lt;60** that requires deep refactor, not patches.
3. **Your product changed** but the site still shows what you were 3 years ago.
4. **Mobile traffic >60%** but site was designed mobile-second.
5. **Your brand evolved** and the web no longer communicates who you are.
6. **Stack is unmanageable**: every change breaks three things.

## False signals

- "I saw a competitor with a nicer look".
- "The CEO wants something new".
- "A new visual trend launched".
- "I don't like my previous designer".

## Total redesign vs sprint evolution

**Total redesign** (3–6 months, $50k–$300k):
- Justified when 3+ real signals are present.
- Risk: temporary SEO drop if executed poorly.

**Sprint evolution** ($5–15k/month):
- Better for 1–2 isolated signals.
- Each sprint measures impact, drops what doesn't work.
- Compound improvement without cutover risk.

## What to measure to justify

Before investing, baseline:
- Global and per-channel conversion
- Bounce rate per main landing
- Time-to-purchase / time-to-lead
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Visitor NPS (if applicable)

If none of these move 6 months post-redesign, it was cosmetic.

## Success case

B2B client with 2019 site. Lighthouse 42, lead conversion 0.8%. Redesign focused on performance + clarity of value + visible case studies.

**Result in 90 days**: Lighthouse 94, lead conversion 2.4% (+200%), organic traffic +35% from CWV improvement.
