Your site loads in 4 seconds and you think it's fine. It isn't. Every extra second is costing you measurable money — in conversion, SEO ranking and brand perception.

## The numbers that hurt

- **+1s of load** = -7% conversion (Akamai).
- **+3s of load** = +32% probability of abandonment (Google).
- **Sites under 2.5s LCP** rank better than slower ones with same content.
- **Mobile**: 53% abandons if a page takes more than 3s.

## The 5 most common causes

### 1. Unoptimized images
4MB PNG uploads are the norm. Solution: WebP/AVIF, srcset, lazy loading, explicit dimensions.

### 2. Heavy JavaScript
Old frameworks, untreeshaken bundles, unnecessary polyfills. Audit with Lighthouse and remove unused.

### 3. Shared or distant hosting
US server selling to LATAM = added latency a CDN for assets alone won't fix.

### 4. Plugins loading everything everywhere
Common in WordPress: 30+ plugins active loading JS/CSS on home even when only used on checkout.

### 5. No CDN or cache
HTML, images and assets served from origin every time. Solution: edge CDN (Cloudflare, Vercel, Fastly).

## Metrics that actually matter

- **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint): &lt;2.5s target.
- **INP** (Interaction to Next Paint): &lt;200ms.
- **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift): &lt;0.1.

These are the **Core Web Vitals** Google uses for ranking.

## 30-day remediation plan

**Week 1**: measure. Lighthouse + PageSpeed Insights + WebPageTest. Per-template baseline.
**Week 2**: images and fonts. Highest-impact quick wins.
**Week 3**: JS — remove unused, code split, defer.
**Week 4**: cache, CDN, correct headers.

Typical result: Lighthouse 50–60 → 85–95. Conversion: +12 to +25%.
