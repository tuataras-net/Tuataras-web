**Schema markup** is code invisible to users but crucial to Google: you explicitly tell it what kind of content each page is. Result: **rich snippets** that boost CTR 20–40% without changing your position.

## What structured data is

Standardized markup (Schema.org) added to your HTML — usually in JSON-LD — describing type, properties and relationships. Google uses it to:

- Show rich snippets (stars, prices, FAQs).
- Enable special features (knowledge panels, AI overviews).
- Understand content more deeply for ranking.

## Schemas a B2B site must have

### 1. Organization
Your company data: name, logo, social, address. Appears in Google's Knowledge Panel when searching your brand.

### 2. WebSite + SearchAction
Enables in-Google "search box" that searches your site.

### 3. BreadcrumbList
Breadcrumbs in results, improving CTR and hierarchy comprehension.

### 4. Article / BlogPosting
For all editorial content. Enables "Top stories" and similar.

### 5. FAQPage
For FAQ pages. Questions appear expandable directly in Google. Significant CTR boost.

### 6. Product (if selling online)
Price, availability, reviews. Essential for e-commerce and SaaS.

### 7. SoftwareApplication
If your product is software/SaaS. Ratings, price, screenshots.

### 8. Service
For professional services pages. Areas served, service type.

### 9. Event
If you organize webinars, conferences, training.

### 10. Review / AggregateRating
If you have testimonials and reviews. Stars in search results.

## Example: Article schema

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "Article title",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Your Company"
  },
  "datePublished": "2026-02-13",
  "image": "https://yourdomain.com/image.jpg"
}
```

Goes inside `<script type="application/ld+json">` in the `<head>`.

## How to implement well

### In static / Next.js sites
Dynamic JSON-LD generation per page type. Reusable component taking props.

### In WordPress
Plugins: Yoast SEO, Rank Math, Schema Pro. Configuration per content type.

### In e-commerce
Shopify, WooCommerce already generate product schema. Validate and extend.

## How to verify it works

1. **Google Rich Results Test**: paste URL, see detected schemas and preview.
2. **Schema Markup Validator** (Schema.org).
3. **Search Console** → Enhancements: shows detected schemas and errors.

## Common mistakes

- **Fake schema**: marking nonexistent reviews, products you don't have. Google penalizes.
- **Outdated schema**: JSON-LD price differs from visible. Keep both in sync.
- **Schema without visible content**: Google wants marked data visible to users.
- **Multiple conflicting schemas**: use one clear per page.
