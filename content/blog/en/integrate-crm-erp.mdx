Your sales team lives in the CRM. Admin team lives in the ERP. If they don't talk, manual data copying costs more than integration. Here's how to do it right.

## The 4 integration options

### 1. Native connectors
HubSpot ↔ NetSuite, Salesforce ↔ SAP, Pipedrive ↔ QuickBooks. If it exists, usually most cost-effective.
**Cost**: $0–$500/mo extra.

### 2. iPaaS (integration platform as a service)
Workato, Tray.io, Boomi, Mulesoft. Platforms for complex integrations without code.
**Cost**: $1k–$15k/mo by volume.

### 3. No-code / low-code
Make, n8n, Zapier for simple to mid cases.
**Cost**: $50–$500/mo.

### 4. Custom
Dedicated dev via APIs. Full flexibility, your own maintenance.
**Cost**: $30k–$150k upfront + maintenance.

## How to choose

| Your situation | Recommended |
|---|---|
| Mainstream systems + standard flows | Native connector |
| Multiple systems + transformation logic | iPaaS |
| Few flows, small budget | No-code |
| Unique logic + high volume | Custom |

## Flows you must integrate

### Lead → Customer
- Deal closes in CRM → creates customer record in ERP.
- Data: name, tax ID, contacts, terms.
- **Without it**: manual re-typing, tax errors.

### Customer → Billing
- ERP issues invoice → CRM sees it attached to deal.
- Payment status returns to CRM.
- **Without it**: sales doesn't know who paid.

### Product / catalog
- ERP source of truth for products, prices, stock.
- CRM syncs for quoting.
- **Without it**: outdated quotes.

### Forecast → Production
- CRM pipeline projects demand.
- ERP/MRP plans procurement and production.

## Technical decisions that matter

### Source of truth per entity
For each data point (customer, product, account), one is source of truth. Other reads. Without this, divergence guaranteed.

### Sync: real-time vs batch
- **Real-time** (webhooks, events): for operationally critical data (e.g., stock).
- **Batch** (every N min/hours): for reports and aggregates.
- Mix by criticality — not everything needs to be instant.

### Error handling
Every integration eventually fails. Design:
- **Retries** with exponential backoff.
- **Dead letter queue** for manual review.
- **Alerts** when errors exceed tolerance.
- **Idempotency**: re-processing same event doesn't duplicate.

## Fatal mistakes

- **Sync everything**: more data, more breakage. Start with critical.
- **No documented field map**: nobody knows why "country" in one is "country_iso2" in other.
- **No observability**: don't know what runs, when, what fails.
- **Strong coupling**: change in one breaks the other.

## Implementation plan

**Month 1**: discovery. Critical data and flow mapping. Source of truth definition.

**Month 2**: most-critical flow integration. Staging tests. Limited go-live.

**Month 3**: expansion to next flows. Monitoring, adjustments.

**Month 4+**: operation, continuous improvement.

## Real case

Distribution company with HubSpot + SAP B1 connected via Excel. 2 people full-time copying data. Monthly tax errors.

Post-integration (iPaaS, 3 months): 0 human hours on sync, errors essentially eliminated, sales has real-time payment visibility.

**ROI**: 7-month payback, estimated annual savings $80k.
