Generative AI moved from lab curiosity to a tool reshaping how every modern company operates. In 2026, we no longer ask whether to adopt AI — we ask **how and where**. This guide grounds the conversation with real use cases, real costs and metrics that matter.

## Why 2026 is the year of serious adoption

Large language models (LLMs) crossed three critical thresholds: enterprise-grade accuracy, reasonable cost, and on-premise availability for sensitive data. That unlocked use cases still experimental in 2023.

**Result**: per Gartner, 78% of mid- and large-sized companies have at least one generative-AI use case in production.

## The 4 highest-ROI use cases

### 1. Augmented customer support
It's not about replacing humans — it's giving them **superpowers**. Agents get suggested replies, prior-ticket summaries and real-time translation. Typical reduction: 40–60% in mean time to resolution.

### 2. Internal analytics and reporting
Talk to your data in natural language. A manager asks "what's our 90-day churn by segment?" and gets a verifiable answer. Tableau and Power BI ship this out of the box.

### 3. Content and marketing generation
Briefs, first drafts, A/B test variants, translation to 20 languages, image generation. **Your creative team isn't replaced — they iterate 5x faster.**

### 4. Code assistants and technical docs
Copilot, Cursor and Claude Code are cutting routine dev tasks 30–55%. The question is no longer whether to use them — it's how to govern them.

## The risks you must mitigate

- **Hallucinations**: LLMs invent facts confidently. Solution: RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) over your source-of-truth.
- **Data leaks**: never send sensitive data to public APIs without DPA. Consider private models.
- **Bias**: audit outputs for decisions affecting people (HR, credit, etc.).
- **Vendor lock-in**: design with abstractions so you can swap models in weeks, not months.

## How to measure ROI

Don't measure "usage" — measure **business impact**. Right metrics:

| Use case | Key metric |
|---|---|
| Customer support | Resolution time, CSAT |
| Marketing | Conversion, cost per lead |
| Operations | Hours saved per process |
| Engineering | Lead time, deploys/week |

## The 90-day roadmap

1. **Days 1–30**: identify 3 high-volume repetitive processes. Measure baseline.
2. **Days 31–60**: pilot one of them. Model, prompt, RAG if applicable. Measure.
3. **Days 61–90**: scale the winner, drop the others without guilt.
