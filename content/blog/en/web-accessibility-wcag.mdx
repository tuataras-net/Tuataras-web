In 2026, accessibility went from "nice to have" to legal obligation in the EU, US and increasingly Latin America. And the market it opens — 1.3 billion people with some disability — makes the ROI obvious.

## Regulatory framework now in force

- **European Accessibility Act**: mandatory since June 2025 for e-commerce, banking, transport, consumer software.
- **ADA (US)**: DOJ confirmed WCAG 2.1 AA as minimum standard for public sites. Lawsuits up 320% since 2020.
- **Latin America**: Mexico, Argentina and Chile have specific laws referencing WCAG.

## WCAG 2.2: what's new and matters

It added 9 new criteria over 2.1. Most relevant:

- **Focus appearance** (2.4.11): keyboard focus indicator must be clearly visible.
- **Target size minimum** (2.5.8): tappable buttons/links must be at least 24x24px.
- **Drag movements** (2.5.7): any drag action must have single-pointer alternative.
- **Accessible authentication** (3.3.8): no cognitive captchas as the only option.

## The 4 mistakes that break 80% of sites

1. **Insufficient contrast** in secondary text, placeholders and links. Minimum: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large.
2. **Images without alt** or with generic alt ("image1.jpg"). Screen readers go blind.
3. **Forms without labels** properly associated to inputs. Assistive tech doesn't know what to ask.
4. **Sites that don't work with keyboard**: Tab navigation must reach everything in logical order with visible focus.

## Pragmatic remediation plan

### Week 1: Audit
- **Automated tools**: axe DevTools, Lighthouse, WAVE. Catch ~30% of issues.
- **Manual testing**: keyboard navigation, screen reader (VoiceOver, NVDA), 200% zoom.
- **Document findings** by severity (blocker, high, medium).

### Weeks 2–4: Critical fixes
Hit blockers first: contrast, alt on key images, forms, keyboard nav. This usually covers 70% of legal risk.

### Month 2: Foundational fixes
Refactor components to use proper semantic HTML. Replace `<div onclick>` with `<button>`. Use ARIA only where native HTML falls short.

### Month 3+: Culture
- Automated accessibility tests in CI (axe-core).
- Linters that block PRs with AA violations.
- Design and engineering training.

## The commercial case (not legal)

- Accessible = better SEO (semantic HTML, alt, structure).
- Accessible = better UX for everyone (captions help in noisy places).
- Accessible = additional market. ~15% of population has some disability. Excluding 1 in 7 customers?
