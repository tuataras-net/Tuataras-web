Build your own LMS or use Moodle/Canvas/TalentLMS? It's the question we get every week. The right answer depends on your business model, not trend.

## Quick comparison

| Criterion | Moodle / Canvas | Custom LMS |
|---|---|---|
| Time-to-launch | 1–3 months | 4–8 months |
| Initial cost | $5k–$30k setup | $40k–$200k+ |
| Recurring cost | $0–$20k/year | Hosting + maintenance |
| Customization | Limited by platform | Total |
| Branding | Templates | 100% own |
| Integrations | Plugins (limited) | Any via API |
| UX | Generic | Designed for your case |

## When to pick Moodle/Canvas

- Traditional educational institution with standard cases.
- Priority is proven functionality over differentiation.
- No product team to maintain custom LMS.
- Need to launch in &lt;90 days.

## When to pick custom

- Your **training product is the business** — the platform is part of the value.
- You have unique pedagogical flows no LMS supports.
- Brand and UX are key differentiators.
- You want to leverage your data without black-box constraints.
- You sell to enterprises demanding white-label, SSO or specific integrations.

## The hybrid path (most used in 2026)

**Headless LMS** or **API-first**: use a base engine (TalentLMS API, Open edX, Canvas LTI) but build your own custom frontend. Combines platform speed + UI flexibility.

## What tilts the balance

**Business strategy**:
- Is the platform **the product** or a **channel**?
- How valuable is your iteration time?
- Does your end customer pay for the platform's brand?

If platform is central, custom pays. If just a course distribution channel, standard usually suffices.

## Real case

B2B corporate training client tried 18 months with TalentLMS. Limits: couldn't customize reports for enterprise clients, generic branding triggered objections, SSO integration was fragile.

Migration to custom LMS (5 months, $140k). Year 1 result: +60% enterprise client retention, +35% avg ticket, NPS +28 points.
