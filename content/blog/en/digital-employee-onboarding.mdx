Bad onboarding costs. Per Gallup, **20% of new hires leave within first 45 days** if early experience is poor. Those who stay take longer to produce. Here's how modern digital onboarding works.

## The 3 dimensions

1. **Administrative**: accounts, accesses, equipment, contracts.
2. **Cultural**: values, people, how we work.
3. **Operational**: how I do my actual job.

Most fail in (2) and (3). Focusing only on one = lopsided onboarding.

## The 90-day plan

### Day -7 to 0 — Pre-boarding
- Welcome email with personal video from manager.
- Access to welcome portal: org chart, mini company history, FAQ.
- Equipment shipped home with kit (laptop, swag, handwritten note).
- First-week calendar visible.

### Day 1 — First impression
- Accounts ALREADY created (not "we'll set up this morning").
- Buddy assigned, not just manager.
- Lunch or coffee chat with team.
- 0 operational meetings. Just meeting people.

### Days 2–14 — Cultural immersion
- Daily 15-min microlearning: history, products, key clients.
- 1:1s with key stakeholders (5–8 people).
- Shadow senior colleagues in their meetings.

### Days 15–45 — Operational ramp-up
- Role-specific learning roadmap.
- First small project with buddy.
- Weekly manager feedback.
- Mid-point check: anything unexpected?

### Days 46–90 — Productivity
- First solo project with safety net.
- Light 360 feedback.
- Formal 90-day review.
- Development plan for next 6 months.

## Ideal tech stack

- **HRIS**: BambooHR, Hibob, or current as source of truth.
- **Onboarding portal**: WorkRamp, Eduflow, or custom LMS.
- **Automation**: n8n / Zapier for triggers (account creation, buddy assign, reminders).
- **Microlearning**: capsules in Synthesia, Loom, no long text.
- **Survey tools**: Officevibe, CultureAmp for weekly pulse checks.

## Metrics that tell you it's working

- **Time to first productive contribution**: days to first real deliverable.
- **Onboarding NPS** (at day 30).
- **Retention at 90 and 180 days**.
- **Qualitative feedback**: "what would have helped?".

## Common mistakes

- "Here are 80 documents, read everything this week."
- Manager absent first week.
- Zero feedback process until annual review.
- Nominal buddy, not really involved.
