"Training is important" doesn't convince the CFO. Measurable ROI does. Here's how to build the business case that justifies real investment in corporate training.

## The Kirkpatrick model (updated to 2026)

Four levels, in order of complexity:

### Level 1 — Reaction
Did they like it? Post-course survey (NPS, CSAT).
**Good for**: quick feedback. **Not ROI**, but input.

### Level 2 — Learning
Do they know more? Pre-test vs post-test.
**Good for**: validating content taught something.

### Level 3 — Behavior
Do they apply it at work? Observation, manager surveys, operational metrics.
**Good for**: confirming transfer.

### Level 4 — Results
Did it move business KPIs? Sales, churn, cycle time, quality.
**This is ROI.**

## The formula that works

```
ROI (%) = ((Benefit - Total cost) / Total cost) × 100
```

**Total cost** includes:
- Content design and production
- Platform (LMS, tools)
- Employee time in training (yes, it counts)
- Facilitator time
- Overhead (management, reporting)

**Benefit** is calculated against the moved KPI:
- Sales: incremental attributable revenue
- Operations: error/hour savings
- Retention: replacement cost savings

## How to attribute results to training

The big challenge. Three options, in order of rigor:

### A. Control group (best)
Half the team gets training, half doesn't. Compare KPI 90–180 days later. Gold standard.

### B. Pre-post comparison
Baseline 90 days before vs 90 days after training. Must normalize for seasonality and other factors.

### C. Manager survey (weaker)
"How much of the change in X do you attribute to training?" — biased but fast.

## Real case

**Program**: closing techniques training for 60 B2B sales reps.
- **Total cost**: $48,000 (content + platform + time).
- **KPI**: qualified opportunity close rate.
- **Pre**: 22% baseline.
- **Post (90 days)**: 28% (trained group) vs 22.5% (control).
- **Attributable delta**: 5.5 points = ~$340k incremental revenue.
- **ROI**: ((340k - 48k) / 48k) × 100 = **608%**.

## Minimum viable dashboard

To present to CFO or board:

1. **Total annual training spend**.
2. **Level 1–2 metrics** per program (engagement, learning).
3. **Level 3 behavior** via manager surveys.
4. **Top 3 programs with calculated Level 4 ROI**.
5. **What's next**: upcoming programs and expected KPIs.

## Mistakes that invalidate measurement

- No KPI defined before the program.
- Zero baseline (can't calculate delta without start point).
- Including only visible costs (excluding employee time).
- Over-attributing (training + new product + favorable market).
