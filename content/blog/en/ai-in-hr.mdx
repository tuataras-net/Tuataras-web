AI in HR is delicate territory — it promises efficiency but can amplify bias and bring legal trouble. Done well, it frees HR to focus on the human side. Done wrong, a candidate sues you.

## Highest-ROI use cases

### 1. Candidate pre-screening
AI reads CVs and ranks against job description. **It doesn't reject** — it ranks. Final decision always human.

**Impact**: review 200 CVs in 10 min vs 2 days.

### 2. Job description generation
Takes inputs (role, level, context) and generates inclusive, clear, brand-consistent versions.

### 3. Personalized onboarding
AI assistant answering new-hire questions 24/7 about policies, tools, procedures. Reduces IT and HR load.

### 4. Pulse survey analysis
Process thousands of qualitative comments, group by theme, detect early signals of dissatisfaction.

### 5. Development recommendations
Based on performance reviews and skills, AI suggests learning paths and internal opportunities.

### 6. Manager virtual coach
Assistant helping managers prep 1:1s, write constructive feedback, handle hard conversations.

## Non-negotiable risks

### Algorithmic bias
If you train on historical data with bad hiring (gender, age, origin bias), AI replicates it. Fairness audits are mandatory.

### Regulatory compliance
- **EU AI Act**: certain HR uses of AI are "high-risk" requiring audits.
- **GDPR**: candidate's right to know why they were rejected.
- **Local**: country-specific labor laws.

### False negatives
A good candidate rejected by algorithm is invisible. If you only measure what passes, you don't know what you missed.

### Privacy
CVs and employee data are sensitive. Never use in public models without DPA. Consider on-prem processing for critical cases.

## Recommended framework

### Layer 1: Assist, don't decide
AI suggests, ranks, summarizes. **Human always decides** things impacting people (hiring, firing, promotion).

### Layer 2: Continuous audit
- What demographic groups pass/fail automatic filters?
- Is there statistical disparity not explained by merit?
- Who's responsible for reviewing?

### Layer 3: Transparency with candidates
Communicate AI use in process. Allow reasonable opt-out. Explain decision process.

### Layer 4: Documentation
All automated decision-support must be documented. Compliance audits will request it.

## 2026 stack

- **Greenhouse / Lever / Ashby**: ATS with integrated AI.
- **Eightfold / Phenom**: AI talent intelligence.
- **Custom assistants** with Claude/GPT + RAG over policies and anonymized data.
- **Workday / SAP SuccessFactors**: AI modules in HRIS.

## Cases NOT to use AI

- Disciplinary decisions.
- Final candidate evaluation for executive roles.
- Mental or emotional health analysis of employees.
- Facial recognition in offices.

## Real case

800-employee services company. Implementation:
- AI CV pre-screening (rank, not reject).
- Onboarding assistant with RAG over internal policies.
- Survey analysis with NLP.

**Year 1 result**: time-to-hire -42%, onboarding NPS +18 points, 3 early flight-risk alerts (addressed in time).
