Implementing the wrong ERP can cost millions and block your operation 18 months. The right decision depends less on the software name and more on your industry, scale and pace of change.

## The 4 real options

| ERP | Sweet spot | Typical investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Odoo** | SMBs / simple mid-market | $30k–$200k |
| **NetSuite** | Growing companies | $80k–$500k |
| **SAP** | Enterprise / corporation | $500k–$10M+ |
| **Custom** | Businesses with unique processes | $150k–$1M+ |

## When standard ERP fits

- Processes are **industry-standard** (manufacturing, retail, services).
- Want to implement fast (3–9 months).
- Compliance needs already covered by ERP.
- Priority is predictability over differentiation.

## When custom fits

- Your **process is competitive advantage** and customizing SAP destroys it.
- You operate in a niche industry without a good module.
- Business model changes fast and needs engineering flexibility.
- Standard ERP customizations would exceed building custom cost.

## The 60/40 rule

Rule of thumb: if **a standard ERP covers 60%+ of your processes without customization**, pick standard and customize the rest.

If it requires modifications touching >40% of core, consider custom — every SAP/Odoo customization is upgrade-future debt.

## Hidden costs of standard ERP

- **Per-user licenses** scaling with company.
- **Customizations** breaking with each upgrade.
- **External consultants** expensive and few.
- **Vendor lock-in**: leaving costs so much you accept price hikes.

## Hidden costs of custom ERP

- **Deep discovery** before coding.
- **Dedicated maintenance team** or trusted partner.
- **Clear roadmap**: without it, becomes Frankenstein.
- **No community**: you're the only reference.

## The hybrid path (increasingly common)

- **Core in standard platform** (Odoo, NetSuite) for finance + inventory + procurement.
- **Custom verticals** integrated via API for differentiating processes (custom CRM, custom fulfillment, pricing engine).
- **Common data layer** centralizing analytics.

Gives you: standard where you don't want to reinvent, custom where it differentiates.

## The right decision process

1. **Map your processes** current and 2-year forward.
2. **Identify the 3–5 critical** ones that are competitive advantage.
3. **Evaluate candidate ERPs** against those specific criticals.
4. **Calculate 5-year TCO**, not initial cost.
5. **Talk to real customers** of the chosen ERP in your industry.

## Fatal mistakes

- Buying first, mapping processes after.
- Underestimating cultural change of ERP.
- Implementation without strong executive sponsor.
- Trying to migrate everything day 1 (always phased).
