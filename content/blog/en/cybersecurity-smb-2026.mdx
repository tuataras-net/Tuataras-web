43% of cyberattacks target SMBs. 60% of those companies close within 6 months of a breach. The good news: reasonable protection costs less than a single incident.

## The most common 2026 threats

1. **AI-powered phishing**: emails and voice deepfakes convincing enough to fool senior staff. The entry point of 90% of attacks.
2. **Ransomware-as-a-Service**: any criminal with $200 can rent a full kit. The price of crime dropped.
3. **Supply-chain attacks**: compromise a vendor to reach you. SolarWinds, MOVEit, Okta cases.
4. **Credential stuffing**: leaked passwords from other sites used to breach yours.
5. **Shadow IT**: staff using SaaS without IT knowing. Each one is attack surface.

## The minimum viable security plan

### Layer 1: Identity
- **MFA everywhere** (email, SaaS, VPN, repos). Non-negotiable.
- **Corporate password manager** (1Password, Bitwarden Business). Goodbye to "Company2026!".
- **SSO** wherever possible. Single source of truth for identities.

### Layer 2: Endpoint
- **Modern EDR** (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Defender). Catches what legacy AV misses.
- **Disk encryption** on every device.
- **Auto-update policy** for OS and browsers.

### Layer 3: Network and data
- **Immutable backups** with 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite. Tested monthly.
- **Network segmentation**: production isolated from dev and from guest WiFi.
- **DNS filtering** (Cloudflare Gateway, NextDNS) blocks phishing before it reaches users.

### Layer 4: People
- **Quarterly training**, not annual. Ideally with phishing simulations.
- **Written incident response plan**, even if just one page.
- **Direct IT channel** to report suspicions without blame.

## Realistic costs

For a 50-employee SMB, a decent setup costs:

| Item | Approx monthly cost |
|---|---|
| MFA + SSO | $150–$400 |
| EDR | $400–$800 |
| Cloud backup | $200–$500 |
| DNS filtering | $100–$250 |
| Training | $200/mo avg |
| **Total** | **$1k–$2k/mo** |

Compared to the average incident cost ($120k–$500k per IBM), ROI is obvious.

## Common mistakes

- "We're too small to be targeted" — you're easier, not invisible.
- "I have antivirus" — AV in 2026 is the floor, not the ceiling.
- "My people wouldn't click that" — they do. All of them. It's measured.
