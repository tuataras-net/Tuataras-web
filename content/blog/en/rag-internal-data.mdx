ChatGPT doesn't know your company. For an LLM to answer using your data — policies, manuals, cases, contracts — you need **RAG** (Retrieval Augmented Generation). It's the key pattern bringing generative AI into enterprise production.

## How RAG works in 1 minute

1. Take your documents (PDFs, wikis, tickets, manuals).
2. Split into chunks and convert to **embeddings** (numeric vectors).
3. Store in a **vector database**.
4. When user asks, find most semantically similar chunks.
5. Pass those chunks to the LLM as context + the question.
6. LLM responds **based on your data**, not its training.

Result: precise, citable, hallucination-free answers.

## Real use cases

- **Internal support**: "What's the LATAM travel policy?"
- **Customer support**: "Does my plan include Excel export?"
- **Sales**: "Summarize last 3 months of interactions with client X"
- **Compliance**: "What documents are needed for vendor onboarding in LATAM?"
- **Onboarding**: "Explain how billing works in our system"

## 2026 stack

### Vector databases
- **Pinecone**: hosted, easy, good to start.
- **Weaviate / Qdrant**: open source, self-hosted or cloud.
- **pgvector**: if you already use Postgres, simplest.

### Embeddings
- **OpenAI text-embedding-3**: balanced price/quality.
- **Cohere Embed v3**: strong multilingual.
- **Open source**: BGE, GTE for sensitive on-prem cases.

### Frameworks
- **LangChain / LlamaIndex**: fast orchestration.
- **Custom**: for full control.

### LLMs
- **Claude / GPT / Gemini** via API.
- **Open source** (Llama, Mistral) for very sensitive data.

## Decisions that matter

### Chunk size
- Too small (200 chars): fragmented context, poor answers.
- Too big (4000 chars): diluted relevance, high cost.
- **Sweet spot**: 500–1500 chars with 100–200 overlap.

### Top-k retrieval
- Few (k=2): may miss needed info.
- Many (k=10+): noise, expensive tokens.
- **Sweet spot**: k=3–5 with re-ranking.

### Re-ranking
After initial search, a model re-orders by real relevance (Cohere Rerank, BGE Reranker). Significantly improves quality.

### Hybrid search
Combine semantic (vectors) + lexical (BM25). Catches cases where exact word matters (codes, IDs).

## Quality-killing mistakes

- **Bad structuring**: scraping PDFs without cleaning headers/footers/numbering.
- **No metadata**: not leveraging tags, dates, author for filtering.
- **Stale embeddings**: docs change, embeddings don't regenerate.
- **No observability**: not measuring response quality, can't improve.
- **Not showing sources**: user can't verify.

## The reliable response pattern

A good RAG response has:
1. **Clear, concise answer**.
2. **Cited sources** (with link to original doc).
3. **Disclaimer if confidence is low**: "couldn't find exact info, this is closest".
4. **Drill-down option**: "want me to search more?".
