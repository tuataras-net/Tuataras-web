Changing 3 words can move conversion more than redesigning the entire homepage. The discipline is **UX writing**, and it's probably the highest ROI in digital product when done right.

## Why it matters so much

Users read 28% of page text on average. Those words decide if:
- They trust you
- They understand what to do
- They take the next step

Each word is gold real estate. Wasting it is expensive.

## Principles that move the needle

### 1. Specific beats generic
- ❌ "Get started"
- ✅ "Create my free account"

### 2. Benefit before feature
- ❌ "Advanced notification system"
- ✅ "Find out before your customer does"

### 3. Active verb in CTA
- ❌ "More info"
- ✅ "Watch 2-min demo"

### 4. Human error message
- ❌ "Error 422: invalid input"
- ✅ "The email looks like it has a typo. Did you mean...?"

### 5. Reassuring confirmation
- ❌ "Operation successful"
- ✅ "Done. You'll get the receipt in 2 minutes."

## Framework to review copy

For each text in your product, ask:
1. Is it clear **what** it is and **what it does**?
2. Is it clear **what happens if I click**?
3. Does it have a human voice or sound like a tech manual?
4. Is it as short as possible without losing clarity?

If you fail one, there's room to improve.

## The test that changes everything

Take your 5 main CTAs and test variants for 2 weeks. Typical result: winner with +12 to +35% conversion vs original.

## Patterns by product type

**B2B SaaS**: focus on outcome, not feature. "Close monthly reports in 1 hour, not 3 days" > "Analytics dashboard".

**E-commerce**: honest urgency + social proof. "Only 3 left" if real. "240 people bought this week" > "Bestseller".

**Onboarding**: micro-victories. Each step should feel easy and completable.
