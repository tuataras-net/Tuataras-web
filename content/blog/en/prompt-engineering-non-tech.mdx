The difference between someone who gets little from ChatGPT and someone who multiplies their productivity is rarely technical — it's knowing **how to ask**. Here are the patterns that work.

## The R-T-F framework

Probably the most useful:

- **R**ole: give the model a role.
- **T**ask: define the task precisely.
- **F**ormat: specify output format.

**Bad**: "Write an email for my client."

**Good**: "You're a B2B SaaS account manager. Write an email to a client who didn't renew, trying to reopen the conversation. Warm, professional tone. 80–120 words. End with an open question."

## The 7 quality-multiplying patterns

### 1. Give enough context
AI doesn't know what you know. Pass relevant data (client, product, situation) in the prompt.

### 2. Show desired output examples
"Write like this example: [X]". Few-shot prompting yields much better results.

### 3. Ask it to think step by step
"Before answering, identify the 3 most important considerations." Makes the model reason better.

### 4. Limit scope
"Don't include X. Only focus on Y." Reduces wandering.

### 5. Iterate, don't accept first output
First answer is rarely best. Ask for improvements: "more concrete", "less formal", "add an example".

### 6. Ask for multiple options
"Give me 3 different versions." You compare and pick — or ask for fusion.

### 7. Define what NOT to do
"No corporate jargon. No 'synergistic' or 'value-add'." Exclusions work.

## Templates for common cases

### Sales email
> You're a [role]. Write an email to [contact type] about [topic]. Goal: [action I want]. Tone: [adjectives]. Length: [word count]. Include: [elements]. Avoid: [things].

### Document analysis
> Here's a document. 1) Summarize in 5 key points. 2) Identify the 3 main risks. 3) Suggest 3 questions to clarify with the author.

### Brainstorming
> I need 15 ideas for [problem]. I want variety: 5 conservative, 5 ambitious, 5 disruptive. For each: 1-line description + main risk.

### Learn a topic
> Explain [topic] like I'm 12. Then add 3 advanced concepts a professional should know. Close with 5 self-assessment questions.

## Most common mistakes

- **Vague prompt**: "help me with marketing".
- **No context**: model assumes generic.
- **Accepting first response**: it's the worst version.
- **No format requested**: get paragraphs when you wanted a table.
- **Wrong tone**: don't specify register, get neutral/boring output.

## Heavy users' secret

Maintain a personal library of working prompts, organized by use. When similar task comes up, reuse + adapt. Productivity compounds.

## Mistakes with sensitive data

- **Never paste confidential info** in free or consumer versions.
- For sensitive: Enterprise accounts (Claude Team, ChatGPT Enterprise) with DPA.
- Better: private/on-prem models for critical cases.
