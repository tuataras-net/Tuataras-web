Choosing the wrong tech partner costs time, money and market opportunity. Here's the filter we use ourselves when recommending partners to clients in areas we don't operate.

## The 12 questions that filter

### Before the proposal
1. **Can they show 3 cases similar to mine?** Without it, you're their experiment.
2. **Who will be on the project day-to-day?** The senior who sells often isn't who delivers.
3. **What projects do they NOT take?** Those who accept everything are problematic.
4. **What's their client retention rate?** >70% year 2 is good signal.

### About the proposal
5. **How do they break down scope?** Vague = risk. Specific with deliverables = professional.
6. **What's the communication plan?** Cadence, tools, owners.
7. **How do they handle scope changes?** Clear procedure vs "we'll figure out later".
8. **What if something goes wrong?** Guarantees, rework, escalation.

### About the team
9. **Can I meet the actual technical team?** Not just sales lead.
10. **Do they subcontract?** If yes, to whom and with what QA control?
11. **What's their turnover?** Team changing every 6 months = guaranteed chaos.

### About the after
12. **What does handoff and post-launch support look like?** Whoever doesn't think post-launch doesn't think about your success.

## Red flags (run away)

- **No documented processes**: "we do it bespoke each time".
- **Won't let you talk to current clients**.
- **Promises of ridiculously short timelines**.
- **Pricing radically cheaper** than competition (something breaks somewhere).
- **Won't sign clear NDA or IP agreements**.
- **No professional digital presence themselves** (ironic but critical).

## Green flags (proceed)

- **They ask hard questions** before quoting.
- **They have clear technical opinions** (with arguments).
- **They tell you what NOT to do**, not just what they can sell.
- **Their proposal admits uncertainty** and handles it with discovery, not vapor.
- **Their team writes, speaks at events, contributes to open source** (real capability signal).

## The right relationship model

**Bad model**: you say what to do, they execute. Result: if you don't know, they add no value.

**Good model**: you define the problem and expected outcomes. They propose the how, bring expertise, push back when needed. Result: sum is greater than parts.

## Healthy payment structure

- **Deposit**: 20–30% to cover startup.
- **Verifiable milestones**: payments tied to accepted deliverables.
- **Retainer**: 10–15% at end, post-acceptance.
- **Time & materials with monthly cap**: flexibility without risk.

Avoid: 100% upfront, payments without clear deliverables, "we'll coordinate later".

## The pilot test

For large projects (>$100k), request a paid 4–8 week pilot with reduced scope. Saves you months of discovering cultural or technical incompatibility.

## Common selection mistakes

- Decision by **price** alone.
- Decision by **unverified referrals**.
- Decision by **brand** ("they're big, can't be wrong").
- Decision by **speed** ("we start now").

All 4 are recipes for regret.
