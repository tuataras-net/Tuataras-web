"We don't have budget for usability research" is the most expensive excuse a team gives itself. Truth: with $0–$500 you can run valuable tests this week.

## Nielsen's rule still holds

**5 users** detect ~85% of usability issues. You don't need 50. You need 5 well-chosen.

## Methods by budget

### $0 — Hallway tests
Ask a colleague from another team to use your prototype. No tutorial, while you watch silently. Note every doubt and stuck.

**Good for**: spotting obvious blocks and copy issues.

### $0–$50 — Tests with existing customers
Email 10 customers offering 30 min of feedback for a discount or credit. 2–3 accept.

**Good for**: validating improvements with people who know your product.

### $50–$200 — Remote unmoderated tests
- **Maze**: tests with auto metrics, ~$50/mo.
- **UserTesting / Useberry**: real tester pool, ~$30 per test.

**Good for**: validating prototypes before development.

### $200–$500 — Moderated tests with incentive
Recruit 5 target users via LinkedIn or Respondent.io. Pay $50–$80 for 30 min Zoom session.

**Good for**: production product with high stakes.

## The 30-minute protocol

1. **Welcome and warm-up** (3 min): "We're testing X. I'm not testing you, I'm testing the product."
2. **Task 1** (5 min): "Find Y."
3. **Task 2** (5 min): "Buy Z."
4. **Task 3** (5 min): "Cancel your subscription."
5. **Open questions** (10 min): what you liked, didn't, what's missing.
6. **Close** (2 min): thanks.

**Golden rules**: don't guide, don't defend the design, note when they hesitate or ask for help.

## The 3 metrics to record

- **Task completion rate**: % completing without help.
- **Time on task**: avg time.
- **Confusion points**: where they hesitated, asked, erred.

## The most common mistake

Showing the session to stakeholders and debating interpretations. Solution: whoever designed is NOT in the stakeholder debrief. Only patterns repeated in 3+ sessions get discussed.

## When NOT to test

- Before having a navigable prototype.
- Without a clear hypothesis to validate.
- With users who don't represent your real target.
