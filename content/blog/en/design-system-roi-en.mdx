A well-implemented design system cuts development time 30–50% and improves product consistency. Poorly done, it's a pretty repository nobody uses. The difference is in how it's conceived from day 1.

## What it is (and isn't)

**Is**: a living system of components, tokens, patterns and guides your team uses to build consistent product faster.

**Isn't**: a Figma library of nice buttons devs never import.

## When it makes sense

- You have **2+ products** or sections sharing visuals.
- Your team is growing and visual onboarding takes weeks.
- **Visible inconsistency** between screens (5 different blue buttons).
- Product needs to **scale features** without reinventing each sprint.

## When it doesn't

- One small product, team of 3.
- MVP validation phase — you don't yet know what to repeat.
- Main client visually changes with every contract close.

## The 3 maturity levels

### Level 1: Tokens
Color, spacing, typography variables. Synced between Figma and code. **This alone gives 40% of the value.**

### Level 2: Components
Buttons, inputs, cards, modals. Documented, accessible, tested. Single source of truth.

### Level 3: Patterns and guidelines
How to combine, when to use what, examples of right and wrong use. What makes a junior design like a senior.

## 2026 stack

- **Figma** + Variables + Tokens Studio for design.
- **Storybook** + visual tests (Chromatic) for components.
- **Tailwind + shadcn/ui** or **Radix UI** as base if you don't want to build from scratch.
- **Style Dictionary** or **Tokens Studio** to sync tokens between Figma and code.

## Common mistakes

- Building it perfect before using it — never finished.
- No clear owner — goes stale.
- Under-documenting — components get duplicated.
- Top-down without involving who'll use it.

## Measured ROI

SaaS client with 4 products. Pre-DS: 12 weeks per medium feature in each. Post: 4–6 weeks with perfect consistency.

**Initial investment**: ~$40k. **Estimated annual savings**: $200k+ in dev and QA.
