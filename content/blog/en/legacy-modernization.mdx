Your critical system runs on tech few people maintain, support costs grow, and any improvement is blocked. Time to modernize — but doing it without breaking the business is the real challenge.

## The 6 modernization strategies (the 6 Rs)

### 1. Rehost ("lift and shift")
Move as-is to cloud. Fast, low risk, limited benefits.
**Good for**: when time-to-cloud is priority and architecture is reasonable.

### 2. Replatform
Move to cloud with minor changes (managed DB, container).
**Good for**: quick wins without rewriting.

### 3. Repurchase
Replace with commercial SaaS.
**Good for**: standard processes (HR, accounting, basic CRM).

### 4. Refactor
Rewrite preserving equivalent functionality.
**Good for**: business core needing deep modernization.

### 5. Rearchitect
Rethink architecture entirely (monolith → services, on-prem → cloud-native).
**Good for**: systems needing different scaling or fundamental change.

### 6. Retire
Turn it off. Sounds obvious but 15% of legacy systems aren't really used anymore.

## The Strangler Fig pattern

Most used and successful. Idea: instead of replacing the old system overnight, **strangle it gradually**.

1. Build new functionality as separate service.
2. Gateway/API in front decides: old or new system?
3. Migrate features one at a time, in production, with instant rollback.
4. When old has no traffic, turn it off.

Typical timeline: 12–36 months for medium systems. Controlled risk.

## Typical 18-month plan

**Months 1–3 — Discovery**
- Real functionality inventory (vs documentation).
- Dependency and data mapping.
- Agreements on scope, success criteria and rollback.

**Months 4–6 — New foundation**
- Modern stack chosen and validated.
- CI/CD pipeline working.
- First minor feature migrated as test.

**Months 7–14 — Progressive migration**
- One feature per sprint with feature flags.
- Comparative metrics old vs new.
- Controlled coexistence.

**Months 15–18 — Closure**
- Retire last legacy pieces.
- Decommission old infra.
- Postmortem and learnings.

## The real risks

- **Data**: migration is 60% of the underestimated effort.
- **Hidden logic**: docs are never complete. Rules in code nobody remembers.
- **People**: those maintaining legacy sometimes resist — it's their power zone.
- **Compliance**: regulatory reports can break in transit.

## Project-killer mistakes

- Big-bang rewrite (everything overnight).
- Underestimating "legacy works" — inertia is real.
- No executive sponsor sustaining the project in tough moments.
- Changing requirements mid-project without re-planning.

## Real case

Client with critical system in COBOL + AS400 since 1998. Progressive migration to cloud microservices over 22 months.

**Result**: 60% operational cost reduction, 8x faster releases, easier talent acquisition, flexibility for previously impossible new products.
