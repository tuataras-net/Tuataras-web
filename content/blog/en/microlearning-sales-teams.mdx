Your sales team won't dedicate 4 hours to a course. But they will dedicate 5 minutes before a meeting. That's the difference reshaping corporate training: **microlearning**.

## What microlearning really is

Short training modules (3–7 min), focused on **a single learning objective**, accessible at moment of need, optimized for mobile.

**Not**: taking a long course and chopping it. It's redesigning for just-in-time consumption.

## Why it works in sales

- **Fragmented attention**: reps check between meetings, in transit, before pitches.
- **Immediate application**: learn → apply on next call.
- **Retention**: 5 focused min remembered better than 60 wandering min.
- **Motivation**: completing a module on a break feels like a win.

## Formats that work

### 1. Short vertical videos (60–90 sec)
One idea, one technique. TikTok-style, vertical, captioned.

### 2. Interactive flashcards
Product, objection, closing technique. Spaced repetition, gamified.

### 3. AI role-plays
Difficult-customer simulator. Practice → instant feedback → retry.

### 4. Objection capsules
"What to say when they say 'too expensive'?" — 3 minutes, actionable, ready.

### 5. Pre-meeting quizzes
Before a call with prospect from sector X, 5-question quiz on that sector.

## Metrics that matter

| Metric | Traditional course | Microlearning |
|---|---|---|
| Completion rate | 13% | 84% |
| Time-to-competence | 90 days | 35 days |
| Field application | 8% | 42% |
| Quote-to-close | Baseline | +18% |

## Real case: B2B SaaS sales team

80-rep team, traditional training with 22% completion.

**Change**: redesign into 60 5-min capsules, mobile platform, light gamification, team leaderboard.

**Result at 6 months**: 89% completion, new-rep ramp from 12 to 5 weeks, win-rate +14%.

## Stack to implement

- **Authoring**: Articulate 360, Synthesia (AI video), Trainn.
- **Distribution**: mobile LMS (TalentLMS, Sana, EdApp).
- **AI tutoring**: ChatGPT Enterprise or Claude for role-plays.
- **Analytics**: dashboard measuring engagement + sales KPI impact.

## Common mistakes

- Calling it "microlearning" but requiring 30 capsules in sequence.
- No business metric: only measuring completion.
- Content not applied next day.
