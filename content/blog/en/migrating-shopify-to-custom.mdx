Shopify is excellent for starting. But beyond a certain point — revenue, catalog complexity, integrations — its limits cost more than migrating. We'll help you read the signals.

## Signs Shopify is too small for you

- You pay over **$2k/mo on Shopify Plus + apps** with no clear ceiling.
- You need **complex business logic** apps don't cover.
- Your **checkout** is limited to what Shopify allows.
- You sell across **multiple channels** and Shopify is the awkward source of truth.
- Performance: stuck at Lighthouse 60–70.

## Migration options

| Option | Pro | Con |
|---|---|---|
| Headless with Shopify backend | Fast, keep Shopify ops | Still pay Shopify |
| Next.js + Medusa (open source) | Full control, no per-sale fees | More owned infra |
| Next.js + commercetools / Saleor | Enterprise-grade, scalable | License cost |
| 100% custom | Maximum freedom | Larger investment |

## Typical migration project

**Month 1**: discovery. Feature mapping, integrations, data, SEO.
**Months 2–3**: build new frontend, model data, migrate products and customers.
**Month 4**: QA, SEO mirror site comparison, 301 redirects.
**Month 5**: gradual cutover with traffic A/B.

Typical cost for mid-size store (1k–5k SKUs): **$60k–$180k**. ROI via fee savings, conversion improvement and iteration speed.

## What you must NOT lose

- SEO history: every important URL needs its 301.
- Customer data with legal consent.
- Reviews and UGC (Yotpo, Loox).
- Operational integrations (ERP, fulfillment, accounting).

## Common mistakes

- Migrating "just because" without numbers to back it up.
- Underestimating international payment flow complexity.
- Launching without redirects: brutal organic traffic drop.
- No rollback plan if things break.
