A tech audit is like an annual physical for your company: gives you an honest diagnosis of your stack, hidden risks and concrete opportunities. Done right, it's the lowest-risk highest-clarity investment.

## When it's worth it

- **Before a big investment** (redesign, migration, integration).
- **Before M&A** or technical due diligence.
- **When there are symptoms but no certainty**: slowness, recurring bugs, frustrated team.
- **Periodically** (every 18–24 months) for software-dependent companies.
- **When tech leadership changes** and you want objective baseline.

## What a serious audit covers

### 1. Architecture and code
- Patterns, tech debt, code quality.
- Coupling, maintainability, testability.
- Tech stack vs industry standards.

### 2. Infrastructure
- Cloud, costs, observability, scalability.
- Single points of failure.
- Disaster recovery and backups.

### 3. Security
- Attack surface.
- Identity and secrets management.
- Compliance (GDPR, HIPAA, ISO 27001 as applicable).

### 4. Data
- Modeling, quality, governance.
- Sources of truth and cross-system sync.
- Access and privacy.

### 5. Processes
- DevOps maturity (DORA metrics).
- QA, releases, incident management.
- Documentation.

### 6. Team
- Capacity vs demand.
- Critical skill gaps.
- Bus factor (how many know critical thing X?).

## What you receive at the end

- **Executive summary** (5–10 pages, non-technical).
- **Full technical report** with categorized findings.
- **Risk matrix** (probability × impact).
- **Prioritized roadmap** with quick wins and strategic projects.
- **Investment and ROI estimates** per initiative.

## How much it costs

| Type | Investment | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Quick audit (focus area) | $5k–$15k | 1–2 weeks |
| SMB integral audit | $15k–$40k | 3–5 weeks |
| Enterprise audit | $40k–$150k+ | 6–12 weeks |

## How to tell good from bad

**Good**:
- Reviews real code, not just interviews.
- Has clear methodology (frameworks used).
- Gives numbers, not adjectives.
- Distinguishes urgent from important.
- Recommends, doesn't impose.

**Bad**:
- Sells the solution before auditing.
- Generic conclusions applicable to any company.
- No access to real code or infra.
- Result is "you have to redo everything".

## What happens after

The real value is in follow-through. The audit is the map; the journey continues:
- Decision on which initiatives to prioritize.
- Quick wins executed in 30–60 days.
- Strategic projects planned.
- Re-audit at 12–18 months to measure progress.
